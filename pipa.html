Pipa

Pipa is a plucked string instrument with a pear-shaped body and a fretted fingerboard.

The pipa is a four string lute instrument that was introduced during the Northern Wei dynasty between 386-534. Originally played like a guitar, performers began playing the pipa as an upright instrument and plucking the strings. Though this instrument has roots outside of China, it eventually became commonplace in Chinese culture.
