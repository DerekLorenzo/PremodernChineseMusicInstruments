Xun

Xun is an egg-shaped, clay wind instrument with a long history dating back to the Neolithic period.

Hover mouse over flip card

The origins of the xun are unknown. Though myth has it that the earliest xun was made from a hunting tool known as the stone meteor.

The xun is a simple wind instrument with a mouthpiece on the side and several holes facing the top. It is one of the oldes instruments in Chinese history with some reports dating it's origins to before the third century BC. While earlier models only had a single hole, a six hole variant had been introduced during the late third century BC.

The xun is one of the more limited instruments as far as it's musical range. It is typically understood to have a range of approximately one octave. Early instruments were made of bones or stone while later versions were often made of clay.
