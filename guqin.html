Guqin

The guqin is a traditional Chinese string instrument that has been around for over 3,000 years.

Hover mouse over flip card

The qin is one of the oldest musical instruments of China, with many mythical stories regarding it's origin and the prominent members of Chinese history that may have invented it.

Before 2001 BC

Chinese lore states that the earliest qin was created before the end of the third millenium B.C. by Fuxi and Shennong, mythical sages.

1046 BC - 256 BC

Documents show frequent use of the qin in the later Western Zhou and earlier Easter Zhou periods. Earlier versions of the qin appeared to have ten strings

206 - 220

Writings from the Han dynasty show the growing significance of the qin, citing it's use as a means of developing character, improving life, and even communicate with gods.

256 - 316

The seven string version of the qin that is more common in modern times emerges during the Western Jin dynasty.

1368 - 1644

Many different practices and rituals have been developed in relation to the qin with Ming dynasty writers insisting the qin be played in an outdoors setting.
